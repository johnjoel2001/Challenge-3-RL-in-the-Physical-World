Why Reinforcement Learning?

Traditional Pursuit Algorithms

  • Proportional Navigation: works for missiles, fails with obstacles and wind
  • PID Controllers: requires manual tuning per drone type, brittle to perturbations
  • Path Planning (A*, RRT): too slow for real-time 3D pursuit at 5+ m/s

All break down when conditions deviate from their design assumptions.

RL Advantage

  • Learns optimal pursuit from millions of simulated encounters
  • Handles uncertainty: wind, sensor noise, motor degradation
  • Adapts to evasion: trained against evasive targets (figure-8, reactive avoidance)
  • Real-time: single forward pass = action in <10ms
  • Generalizes: domain randomization makes policy robust to unseen conditions
RL Agent:   observation (21-dim) → MLP [256, 256] → thrust (3-dim)   @ <10ms

PPO: Proximal Policy Optimization

Why PPO Specifically?

  • Stable training: clipped objective prevents catastrophic updates
  • Sample efficient: 4 parallel envs, 2048-step rollouts
  • Small memory: on-policy, no replay buffer — runs on student laptops
  • Battle-tested: OpenAI Five (Dota), robotics, most Gymnasium benchmarks

Reward Structure: Teaching the Agent to Kill Efficiently

The reward function encodes our operational objective: intercept fast, avoid obstacles, minimize energy (battery = cost).

Positive Rewards (Do This)

1. Progress Reward+15 × Δd per step

Main gradient signal. Rewards closing distance to target. Zero for orbiting (distance unchanged), so the agent MUST approach.

2. Interception Bonus+500 + speed bonus

Massive terminal reward. Speed bonus = (max_steps - steps_taken) × 0.5. Faster kill = bigger reward. Dominates all cumulative per-step rewards.

3. Proximity Bonus+1/(d+0.3) when d < 3m

Exponential reward within 3m. ~0.5 at 3m, ~3.0 at 0.5m. Rewards commitment to final approach. Capped to prevent orbit-farming.

Negative Rewards (Don't Do This)

4. Time Penalty-0.5 per step

KEY anti-orbiting mechanism. 500-step orbit costs -250. Quick intercept (500 bonus - 100 time cost = +400) is clearly optimal. This is the most important penalty.

5. Collision Penalty-75

Crashing into obstacles = drone replacement cost. Combined with continuous obstacle proximity warning (-3.0 when within 30% of ray range).

6. Energy Penalty-0.02 × ||a||²

Penalizes excessive thrust. Energy = battery = mission cost. Kept small so it doesn't discourage aggressive pursuit.

7. Out of Bounds-75

Leaving the arena = mission failure (lost drone). Same magnitude as collision.

The Sim2Real Gap: What Can Go Wrong

Training in simulation is cheap and safe. But the real world doesn't follow your simulator's assumptions.

Challenges

1. Unmodeled Aerodynamics

Our sim uses point-mass + linear drag. Real drones have rotor wash, ground effect, blade flapping, and vortex ring state. Policy may overshoot in hover-to-sprint transitions.

2. Sensor Latency & Noise

Sim observation is instantaneous and clean. Real GPS has 100ms latency, IMU drifts, camera tracking drops frames. Our action delay randomization (0–3 steps) partially addresses this.

3. Wind & Turbulence

Sim drag is constant. Real wind is spatially varying, gusty, and creates turbulence near buildings. Our drag randomization (0.1–0.6) covers a wide range but not spatiotemporal correlation.

4. Target Behavior

Sim evader uses scripted figure-8 + reactive evasion. Real adversary drones may use GPS waypoints, swarm coordination, or adversarial RL counter-policies.

Our Mitigations

Domain Randomization

8 physics parameters randomized per episode. Policy learns to be robust across 2x range of mass, thrust, drag, speed, obstacles. Real world is "just another sample."

Observation Noise Injection

Gaussian noise (σ 0–0.05) added to observations during training. Makes policy robust to imperfect sensors without explicit sensor modeling.

Action Delay Randomization

0–3 step delay between action decision and execution. Simulates ESC response time, flight controller lag, and compute latency on Jetson.

Conservative Reward Tuning

Energy penalty discourages aggressive maneuvers that only work in sim. Proximity bonus rewards smooth final approach over high-speed collisions.

"Sim2real transfer works not because the sim is accurate, but because domain randomization makes the policy invariant to things the sim gets wrong."
— OpenAI, Learning Dexterous In-Hand Manipulation, 2019
